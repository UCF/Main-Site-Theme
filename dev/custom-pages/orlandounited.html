UCF Stands with Orlando Against Terror

On June 12, Orlando was attacked. This is a tragedy for UCF, Central Florida and the nation. Our hearts go out to all of the victims and their families. It is in our nature as Knights to comfort, console and assist. Learn how you can help.

#OrlandoUnited

The UCF Creed calls on all of us to "promote an open and supportive campus environment by respecting the rights and contributions of every individual."

With our Creed in mind, it is important our LGBTQ students, faculty, staff and alumni know this: You are not alone. Your university stands with you.

We Stand With Orlando

Donate Blood

Already, hundreds of UCF students, faculty, staff and alumni have donated blood to support the victims of this horrific crime. Blood drives will continue on campus through the remainder of the week:
  • 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Wednesday, June 15, one bus will be in front of the Student Union.
  • 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Thursday, June 16, one bus will be in front of the Student Union.
Please visit http://blooddrives-ucf.org for the latest information about blood drives on campus. OneBlood lists other Orlando blood drive locations and offer more information on blood donation eligibility.

Give Back

Throughout the week, organizations at UCF and across Central Florida are hosting drives and volunteer events. Here are ways to get involved:
  • The City of Orlando is collecting contact information from people interested in volunteering, and has established a fund to provide a way to help respond to the needs of our community after the effects of the Pulse tragedy. You can learn more and donate on the OneOrlando site.
  • Equality Florida has established a Pulse Victim Fund for victims and their families.
  • Wednesday, June 15, 3 to 5 p.m. in the Multicultural Student Center, Student Union Room 207, the Black Student Union and RUKUS will be collecting non-perishable items and making postcards for the victims.

Help Heal

To help our students, faculty and staff during the difficult days ahead, UCF will host a series of campus conversations and therapy support sessions to discuss the impact of this tragedy and understand how to cope as a community. Services and resources are available through:
  • UCF Cares
  • UCF Victim Services
  • UCF RESTORES

Campus Conversations:

June 13-17, noon to 1 p.m. in Student Union, Room 313, and 1-2 p.m. at Pride Commons, located in Ferrell Commons 171 across from Marketplace

UCF Writes to Remember:

June 16, 4 to 6 p.m. at the University Writing Center, Colbourn Hall 105, to write as a community after the tragedy

Therapist Support:

June 13-17, 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. at Pride Commons, located in Ferrell Commons 171 across from Marketplace

UCF RESTORES:

Daily at UCF's Center for RESearch and Treatment On Response to Extreme Stressors, Psychology Building 139. Free and open to anyone in the community who needs assistance.
Off campus, the GLBT Community Center of Orlando is working with community partners to provide crisis services to the families, friends and others affected by the shooting. Other community partners offering assistance are Zebra Coalition and Equality Florida.
