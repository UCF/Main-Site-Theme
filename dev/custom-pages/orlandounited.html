UCF Stands with Orlando Against Terror

On June 12, Orlando was attacked. This is a tragedy for UCF, Central Florida and the nation. Our hearts go out to all of the victims and their families. It is in our nature as Knights to comfort, console and assist. Learn how you can help.

#OrlandoUnited

[social-share-buttons]

The UCF Creed calls on all of us to "promote an open and supportive campus environment by respecting the rights and contributions of every individual."

With our Creed in mind, it is important our LGBTQ students, faculty, staff and alumni know this: You are not alone. Your university stands with you.

Thank You Blood Donors

Donate Blood

Already, hundreds of UCF students, faculty, staff and alumni have donated blood to support the victims of this horrific crime. Blood drives will continue on campus over the coming weeks:
  • 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Tuesday, July 5, in front of the Student Union and Veterans Commemorative Site on Memory Mall.
  • 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. Wednesday, July 6, in front of the Student Union.
Please visit http://blooddrives-ucf.org for the latest information about blood drives on campus.
OneBlood lists other Orlando blood drive locations and offer more information on blood donation eligibility.

Give Back

Organizations at UCF and across Central Florida are hosting drives and volunteer events. Here are ways to get involved:
  • The City of Orlando is collecting contact information from people interested in volunteering, and has established a fund to provide a way to help respond to the needs of our community after the effects of the Pulse tragedy. You can learn more and donate on the OneOrlando site.
  • Equality Florida has established a Pulse Victim Fund for victims and their families.

Help Heal

To help our students, faculty and staff, UCF hosted a series of campus conversations and therapy support sessions to discuss the impact of this tragedy and understand how to cope as a community. Services and resources continue to be available to all who need them:
  • UCF Cares
  • UCF Victim Services

UCF RESTORES:

Daily at UCF's Center for RESearch and Treatment On Response to Extreme Stressors, Psychology Building 139. Free and open to anyone in the community who needs assistance.

Employee Assistance Program Resources:

This tragedy has impacted our staff and faculty members. Employees can find more about UCF’s Employee Assistance Program here.
