Medicine

The College of Medicine and the Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences are located at the UCF Health Sciences Campus at Lake Nona. The medical school is an integral part of a new medical city where future doctors will train in close proximity to world-class partners.

Thanks to $6.5 million in community donations, the UCF College of Medicine became the first med school in U.S. history to provide full scholarships to an entire class. The full four-year scholarships for the Charter Class (Class of 2013) allowed students to graduate from a new medical school debt-free and allowed UCF to attract the best and brightest students to a new school.
The college is a research-intensive medical school that is unique nationally in that it includes almost 3,000 undergraduate majors and graduate students in the biomedical sciences through its Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences.
TODO
The college is one of the few in the nation that includes a mandatory two-year research module for all M.D. students. The Focused Inquiry and Research Experience (FIRE) module requires first- and second-year students to do scientific research on a topic about which they are passionate. The goal is to develop in students a "spirit of inquiry" to discover and address patient problems. Because of that FIRE module, many students present and publish research locally, regionally and nationally before ever graduating from medical school.
UCF M.D. students score in the top quartile nationally in academics based on the United States Medical Licensing Step 1 and 2 Exams of basic science and clinical knowledge. For example, the Class of 2016's average score on the Step 2 clinical exam was 253 compared to the national average of 240 (latest data available). The Class of 2017's average score on the Step 1 science exam was 239, compared to the national average of 229 (latest data available). On the National Board of Medical Examiners Subject Exams, UCF students scored above the national average on every subject tested – pediatrics, OB/GYN, neurology, psychiatry, surgery and medicine. UCF's scores have increased even as enrollment grew more than 10-fold in the last seven years.

The college's baccalaureate students routinely exceed state and national averages on the RN license exam (NCLEX-RN), ranking among the top 4% nationally and one of the highest pass rates of equivalent programs in Florida.
In 2015 and 2016, the college was recognized by U.S. News & World Report as one of the nation’s "Best Online Graduate Nursing Programs" and "Best Online Graduate Nursing Programs for Veterans."

The college's Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN), Master of Science in Nursing (MSN), Doctor of Nursing Practice (DNP) and post-graduate certificate programs are nationally accredited by the Commission of Collegiate Nursing Education, which sets high standards for nursing education.
An educational leader in the state, the College of Nursing has been the first in Florida to offer many programs including an online RN to BSN degree, an innovative concurrent ASN to BSN program in partnership with state colleges, an online PhD for nurse scientists and most recently, a Nursing and Healthcare Simulation graduate program.

Among the college's nationally recognized faculty are six endowed chairs, the most at UCF, including a computer scientist to enhance research collaboration across disciplines such as simulation, medicine and engineering. Faculty also include 10 fellows of national nursing organizations, four certified health care simulation educators and an advanced health care simulation educator, one of only 18 certified in the U.S.
