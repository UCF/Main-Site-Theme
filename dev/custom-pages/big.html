At UCF, the second-largest university in the U.S., bigger is better. Being big means more access for people of any age who might not otherwise get an opportunity to earn a high-quality college degree. Being big attracts experts from around the world to conduct innovative research and pass their knowledge on to students. And being big creates positive, lasting impact on the Central Florida community and beyond.

Mentor Magnetism

Growing up in a neighborhood challenged by crime and poverty, Phillip Gordon found sanctuary in a city youth program where mentors steered him away from trouble.

"Nobody around me went to college. I felt proud that they told me, 'You're a good kid. You have to leave here and go to college.' It stuck with me."

On that advice, Phillip went away to college, but homesick, returned a few weeks later. Determined to pursue his dream, he enrolled at Valencia College and registered for the DirectConnect to UCF program. "Going to UCF was the best thing for me." Now, he is a mentor and teacher inspiring children in the same neighborhood where he grew up.

"If you want to see change, you have to work at it and you have to be patient. My ultimate goal is to make an impact on lives, children's lives."
Phillip Gordon, '13, teacher and mentor
