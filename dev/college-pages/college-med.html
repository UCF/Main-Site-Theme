Medicine

The College of Medicine and the Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences are located at the UCF Health Sciences Campus at Lake Nona. The medical school is an integral part of a new medical city where future doctors will train in close proximity to world-class partners.

Thanks to $6.5 million in community donations, the UCF College of Medicine became the first med school in U.S. history to provide full scholarships to an entire class.
The college is a research-intensive medical school that is unique nationally in that it includes almost 3,000 undergraduate majors and graduate students in the biomedical sciences.
UCF M.D. students score in the top quartile nationally in academics based on the United States Medical Licensing Step 1 and 2 Exams of basic science and clinical knowledge.
The college is one of a few in the nation that includes a mandatory two-year research module for all M.D. students, the Focused Inquiry and Research Experience.
On the National Board of Medical Examiners Subject Exams, UCF students scored above the national average on every subject tested: pediatrics, OB/GYN, neurology, psychiatry, surgery and medicine.
